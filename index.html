Distribution and Routes Information of UGGp in Indonesia

GEOPARK

GEO + PARK

Geographical areas which there are geological heritage sites (geosite) and valuable natural landscapes related to aspects:

1. Geological Heritage (geoheritage)

2. Geological Diversity (geodiversity)

3. Biodiversity

4. Cultural Diversity

UNESCO Global Geopark(UGGp)

International earth heritage for sustainable indigenous development investment on the basis of educational conservation.

Functon :

1. Conservation / Protection

2. Education

3. Sustainable Development

UNESCO Global Geopark (UGGp) : 195 Geopark spread across 48 countries in the world.

    Batur Geopark

    It encompasses two volcanic calderas with the 370.5 km2 outer caldera wall surrounding the inner caldera, Mount Batur, and the Lake. There are beautiful landscapes derived from the old (outer) and young (inner) calderas formed from a catastrophic eruption 29,000 years ago. It is an important component of the "Pacific Ring of Fire" with endemic flora, fauna and indigenous Balinese Hindu culture.

    Gunung Sewu Geopark

    Known as a tropical karst area that stretches from Gunung Kidul DIY to Pacitan East Java with an area of 1500km. It has a diversity of geosites, one of which is the Nglanggeran Ancient Volcano Site where there are beautiful springs coming out of rock fissures around the mountains.

    Rinjani Geopark

    Centered on Mount Rinjani as the second highest mountain in Indonesia which has an altitude of 3,726 meters above sea level. The Rinjani crater, also called Segara Muncar, is a beautiful caldera, and there is also Segara Anak Lake formed by volcanic processes with clear blue water.

    Ciletuh Geopark

    Geopark with the concept of sustainable tourism and conservation of land to sea with a variety of geological diversity, flora, fauna, and cultural tourism. Diverse natural beauty, one of which is Curug Puncak Manik which has a height of up to 100m with a clear waterfall shower. There are also other waterfalls such as Cikaso, Awang, and Cigangsa.

    Kaldera Toba Geopark

    The caldera was formed from collapse after the supervulcano eruption of the ancient Toba volcano which then became a volcano-tectonic caldera (giant volcano crater) and was filled by rainwater. Some of the Geosites in the Toba Caldera include Hutatinggi-Sidohini, Haranggaol, Parapat, Hutaginjang, Eden Forest, Batak Museum, and Sipinsur.

    Belitong Geopark

    The Belitong Geopark has three valuable geological heritages, namely satam stones (stones formed from the collision of meteors with layers of earth with high tin content), granite rocks, and the historical heritage of tin mining.In addition, there is also a wide variety of flora and fauna diversity.To date, there are 17 geosites within the Belitong Geopark Area.

    Ijen Geopark

    This geopark area has geological diversity in the form of ijen blue fire, wurung crater, acid lake, blawan lava, and others. There are 21 developed geological sites, ranging from local to international scale. One of the many geological sites that has an extraordinary phenomenon is the Ijen Crater, the most acidic crater lake on earth with blue fire that appears in the Solfatara area. Biological diversity includes rainbow forest and Bondowoso coffee, as well as cultural diversity such as the coffee picking dance.

    Maros Pangkep Geopark

    Nicknamed "The Land Of Tomorow".In this geopark area there are various natural attractions based on geodiversity, geoheritage and biodiversity.There are 26 excellent geosites including rock caves, bantimurang waterfall, malaka village, amarani forest, and others.There is also a diversity of flora, fauna and culture.

    Merangin Geopark

    This geopark area has geological diversity along the Merangin River, as well as a diversity of flora and fauna which are also recorded as ancient fossils around 250 - 290 million years ago spread across 15 attractions in the Merangin Geopark. Some of them are Nilo Sungsang hot spring, black river waterfall, segerincing waterfall, blue telago lake, beetle crater lake, sengayau cave, and fossils such as tuff fossils, leaf fossils, and wood fossils.

    Raja Ampat Geopark

    Nicknamed the "Emerald Karst of the Equator". This area is a group of karst islands and old rocks that are 439 – 360 million years old. In this area there is also megabiodiversity, geoheritage and diverse cultural heritage. Some of them include the karst of Piaynemo, Bat Island, Karst of Wayag, Jellyfish Lake, and Mansuar.
